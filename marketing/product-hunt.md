## Description

Engagement increases 15–50% when a UI switches from pagination to infinite scroll. Platforms know this. It's why the Next Page button disappeared.

RePage puts it back.

Install it and every website gets a numbered nav bar at the bottom of every scrollable area:

**‹ 1 2 3 … 47 ›**

Use **Z / X** (right-hand mouse friendly) or arrow keys to flip pages.

**Works on:** Reddit, Twitter/X, YouTube, Wikipedia, Gmail, ChatGPT, Gemini, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram — any website with a scrollable element.

**Features:**

- Numbered pagination on every feed and article
- Z / X keyboard shortcuts (optimised for right-hand mouse use)
- Sub-scroll containers (Wikipedia TOC, chat sidebars, contact lists) get their own mini nav bar
- Greyscale mode — makes interfaces less visually stimulating
- Disable Autoplay — pauses all videos on load
- Mute Notifications — hides unread badges and counts
- Hide Like Counts — removes engagement metrics
- Text-Only mode — strips images and videos from social feeds

**Privacy:** Zero data collection. No account required. Permissions: activeTab + storage only.

**Free and open source:** https://github.com/codycsmith41-a11y/repage

## Maker comment (post this as the first comment after launch)

Hey PH! 👋

I built RePage after going down a rabbit hole on dark patterns in UI design. The research is stark: engagement increases 15–50% when a site switches from paginated content to infinite scroll. That's a euphemism. It means platforms extract up to 30% more of your time just by removing a "Next Page" button.

This isn't accidental. Infinite scroll mimics the continuous visual flow that signals movement through a physical environment — the same neurological mechanism that motivated exploration in our evolutionary past. Platforms exploit this. [Utrecht University researchers](https://www.uu.nl/en/news/researchers-advise-the-house-of-representatives-education-needed-on-safe-screen-use-for-children) specifically called this out to parliament as a mechanism disproportionately harming children and neurodivergent users.

RePage is the counterweight. Scrolling itself has a role, but infinite scroll — the version that deliberately removes stopping points — is the dark pattern. Putting page numbers back in creates natural decision moments. You finish a page. You decide. That's the entire mechanic.

The extension is step one. The goal is to unscroll all tech — starting with putting the _page_ back in _webpage_.

Would love your feedback, especially on sites where it breaks or a scrollable area gets missed.
