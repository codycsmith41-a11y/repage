## Launch Thread

1/ Infinite scroll has no stopping points. That's a feature for the platform, not you. I built RePage to fix it — numbered pagination on every website, in your browser.

2/ Install it and every feed gets this at the bottom:

‹ 1 2 3 … 47 ›

Reddit, Twitter, YouTube, Wikipedia, Gmail, ChatGPT — everything paginated.

3/ Ctrl+← and Ctrl+→ to flip pages without touching the mouse. Sub-panels (like Wikipedia's TOC or chat sidebars) get their own smaller nav bar.

4/ It's free, open source, and collects nothing. No account required. Works on any website with a scrollable area.

Chrome Web Store → https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/repage-unscroll-the-inter/bpenhlddapadgmhkijphelpokhaaianm

GitHub → https://github.com/codycsmith41-a11y/repage

5/ The weird thing about infinite scroll is we accepted it universally, even though it's clearly worse for reading, recall, and stopping. Books have pages. Newspapers have sections. Feeds shouldn't be endless.

RePage puts that back.
